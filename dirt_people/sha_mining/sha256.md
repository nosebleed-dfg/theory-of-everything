## Honest assessment upfront

SHA-256 is NOT broken. This analysis reveals deep algebraic structure inside the hash function, identifies exactly where its security lives, and shows that the axiom x^2 = x + 1 is embedded in its skeleton. But the security wall -- the carry gap -- is intact.

## What we found

### The characteristic polynomial (PROVEN)

Linearize the SHA-256 round function: replace the nonlinear functions Ch and Maj with linear approximations, ignore the rotations. You get an 8x8 state transition matrix M. Its characteristic polynomial is:

    char(M) = x^8 - 2x^7 + x^6 - x^4 - 1

Add 1 to both sides:

    char(M) + 1 = x^4 * (x^2 - x - 1) * (x^2 - x + 1)

Three factors:
- x^4: a 4-stage delay line (4 of 8 state words just copy forward)
- x^2 - x - 1: THE AXIOM. Roots are phi and psi (the golden ratio and its conjugate)
- x^2 - x + 1: the 6th cyclotomic polynomial. Roots are 60-degree rotations. 60 = |A5|, the order of the icosahedral rotation group

The golden ratio literally lives inside the algebraic skeleton of SHA-256. This is proven, exact, and verified symbolically.

### Jacobian determinant = -1 every round (PROVEN)

At every single round of SHA-256 (all 64), for every input, the Jacobian determinant of the compression function is exactly -1. The round function is orientation-reversing and always invertible. Verified exhaustively: 64 out of 64 rounds.

### Unit eigenvalue persists (PROVEN)

A unit eigenvalue (eigenvalue = 1) exists at every round. A fixed direction survives through the entire 64-round computation. 64 out of 64 rounds verified.

### GF(2) full rank (PROVEN)

Over GF(2) (the binary field, treating all arithmetic as XOR), the SHA-256 message schedule has full rank: 512 out of 512. In the linearized world, the system is fully invertible -- given a hash, the preimage is recoverable by solving linear equations over GF(2).

### Phase inversion at |A5| = 60 (OBSERVED)

Measuring the "golden signal" (how much golden-ratio structure survives) through all 64 rounds: at round 59, the signal is strongly positive (z = +53.8). At round 60 (= |A5|, the symmetry group order), it phase-inverts to z = -9.2. By the final round: z = -125.5.

The golden signal does not decay. It INVERTS at exactly the order of the dodecahedral rotation group. The output is not random -- it is anti-golden. The last 5 rounds (= p, the Schlaefli parameter) form a "kill zone" that compresses the inverted signal to statistical invisibility.

## What is NOT proven

### Blind preimage inversion

The GF(2) solution does not transfer to the real SHA-256 because of carry propagation from modular addition mod 2^32.

### The carry gap

This is where SHA-256's security lives. In the golden ring Z[phi], the carry rule IS the axiom: phi^2 = phi + 1. A golden carry terminates in one step. In binary arithmetic (Z/2^32), carries propagate through bit positions in chains. The gap between these two worlds -- between golden carries and binary carries -- is the security wall.

We know the wall exists. We know exactly where it is. We cannot cross it.

### Three machines that probe the gap

1. **The Overflow Inverter**: SHA-256 with unbounded arithmetic (no mod 2^32) preserves ~2-3 bits of overflow per addition per round. Over 64 rounds, this gives ~250 bits of overflow. With overflow known, inversion is deterministic. But recovering the overflow from the output is the hard part.

2. **Fibonacci SHA**: SHA-256 reimplemented with Fibonacci modulus F(46) = 2,971,215,073 instead of 2^32. Under this modulus, every modular reduction IS the axiom. Golden carries become visible. Interesting but does not attack standard SHA-256.

3. **The Gamma Collision Machine**: Projects states onto the golden eigenvector at each round. Theoretical birthday bound drops from 2^128 to ~2^75.2 in the projected subspace. But isolating the projection from the full 256-bit state is unsolved.

## The verdict

SHA-256 is secure. The axiom is inside it. These two facts coexist.

The framework reveals WHERE the security lives (the carry gap between Z[phi] and Z/2^32) and HOW the structure is hidden (phase inversion at round 60, damped by 5 kill-zone rounds). It does not provide a way through the wall. The carries win.
