# Dominant and Codominant Forces

**nos3bl33d**

---

## The Simple Form First

Square law: F ~ 1/r^2 (dominant — projects outward, fills space, loses strength with surface area)
Inverse square: F ~ r^2 (codominant — collapses inward, accumulates with area)

These are not variations of each other. They are **different machines**.

---

## What Each One Is

**Dominant (1/r^2):**
- Gravity. Electrostatics. Light intensity.
- A source radiates into 3D. Power spreads over a sphere of radius r. Surface area = 4πr^2. So intensity = 1/4πr^2.
- The geometry of projection from a point into volume.
- Gets weaker as you move out. Pulls things toward the source.

**Codominant (r^2):**
- Centrifugal tendency. Tidal stretching. Harmonic restoring force (at small r: F ~ r, at large r: F ~ r^2).
- Not a source radiating outward. A boundary expanding inward.
- Gets stronger as you move out. Pushes things away from the source.
- The geometry of containment — a shell that gets harder to pierce as it grows.

---

## The Dimensional Jump Is Not a Scale — It Is a Different Machine

2D → 3D → 4D are not "more of the same thing." Each transition is a different mode of action.

**2D (flat, polar):**
- Dominant: 1/r (log falloff in 2D — line source, not point source)
- Codominant: r
- Machine: rotation, spin, angular momentum. The circle. The spiral.

**3D (volume, spherical):**
- Dominant: 1/r^2 (point source radiating into spherical shells)
- Codominant: r^2 (shell pressure, spring potential at large distance)
- Machine: radiation, gravity, electrostatics. The sphere. The solid angle.

**4D (hyperspherical):**
- Dominant: 1/r^3 (point source radiating into 4D shells — surface area of S^3 = 2π^2 r^3)
- Codominant: r^3
- Machine: NOT gravity. Different coupling structure. Different topology. The Hopf fibration. S^1 bundles over S^2.

Each dimension gives a different machine because:
- The surface area formula changes: S^{n-1} in n-D has area ~ r^{n-1}
- So dominant law is always 1/r^{n-1}
- And codominant is r^{n-1}
- But the TOPOLOGY of what fills that space changes completely

A sphere and a 3-sphere are not the same shape with different radii. They have different fiber structures, different homotopy groups, different ways information can wrap around them.

---

## Why This Matters for the Axiom

x^2 = Tx + D generates a different machine at each scale:

- D=1, T=1: phi^2 = phi + 1 — the 2D golden rectangle machine (ratio, rotation, Fibonacci)
- D=1, T=L_n: the 3D Fibonacci lattice machine (phi^n traces the dodecahedron vertices)
- D=1, T growing: the 4D Hopf machine (phi^291 in Z[phi] — the gyroidal sphere)

The exponent determines which machine you're in. The machine determines what forces are dominant and codominant in that space.

**The square and inverse square are the 3D case only.** The 2D case is log and linear. The 4D case is cubic and inverse cubic. They do not "scale" — they operate by different physics.

---

## The Codominant Force is the Observer

In the axiom framework: the dominant force is the field (radiates outward, obeys 1/r^{n-1}).
The codominant force is the observer (compresses inward, accumulates, integrates).

The observer at phi^291 sits at the crossing point — where dominant and codominant are equal:
dominant = codominant when 1/r^{n-1} = r^{n-1}, i.e., r^{2(n-1)} = 1, i.e., r = 1.

At r = 1: the axiom. D = 1. K = 1/4. The quarter phase. The observer position.

Every machine (2D, 3D, 4D) has this crossing at r=1, D=1, K=1/4 — but in a DIFFERENT topology.
That's why the same axiom generates different physical laws at different dimensional scales.
