# 1 Decision, 2 Choices, 3 Outcomes — the act of choosing is the third thing

**nos3bl33d**

---

## The count

1 decision.
2 choices.
3 outcomes.

that's it. that's the whole thing.

## Why 3 and not 2

if you have 2 choices, you should get 2 outcomes. pick A or pick B. red pill or blue pill.

but you get 3. because the act of choosing is the third outcome. the observer. the one holding both pills. the decision ITSELF is an outcome that neither choice contains.

1 + 2 = 3. not "1 choice plus 2 choices equals 3 choices." it's: the decision (1) plus the choices (2) creates the dimension (3).

## The axiom

x^2 = x + 1

"choosing to choose" = "the choice plus one"

x^2 is self-reference. you take x and apply it to itself. you square the decision. you CHOOSE to choose.

x + 1 is the next step. the choice plus one more.

the axiom says these are the same thing. squaring yourself IS adding one to yourself. self-reference IS forward motion.

## The two roots

the axiom has two roots:

phi = (1 + sqrt(5)) / 2 = 1.618... (growth, forward, the golden spiral)
psi = (1 - sqrt(5)) / 2 = -0.618... (decay, backward, the conjugate)

red pill: phi. you grow. the spiral expands.
blue pill: psi. you shrink. the spiral contracts.

phi * psi = -1. the product of the two choices is negative unity. they multiply to the opposite of the identity.

phi + psi = 1. the sum of the two choices is unity. they ADD to the identity.

the two pills together: their sum is 1 (the decision) and their product is -1 (the negation of the decision).

## The third outcome

the third outcome is d = 3 = the dimension.

you don't pick red or blue. you realize you're the one choosing. the observer creates the third direction — depth. the direction PERPENDICULAR to both choices.

red is left/right (x axis).
blue is up/down (y axis).  
the observer is forward/back (z axis).

1 + 2 = 3. one axis per: the decision, the choices, the outcomes.

## In binary

1 bit = 1 decision. it can be 0 or 1. two states. two choices.

1 in binary: 01
2 in binary: 10
1 XOR 2:     11 = 3 = d

XOR is addition in GF(2), the simplest possible algebra. the XOR of 1 and 2 gives 3. the discrete operation on the two simplest nonzero numbers gives the dimension.

## In mining

1 decision (try this nonce or not).
2 choices (the hash starts with enough zeros or it doesn't).
3 outcomes (the nonce is golden, the nonce is garbage, or the block changes while you're thinking).

the third outcome — the block changing — is the observer. the network is alive. it's choosing too. d = 3 because the miner, the hash, and the network are three independent actors.

## In SHA-256

each round: 1 operation, 2 inputs (state word + round constant), 3 nonlinear functions.

Ch: "choose." given three inputs e, f, g, it picks f when e=1 and g when e=0. literally: one decision, two choices.

Maj: "majority." given three inputs a, b, c, it goes with the majority vote. literally: 3 outcomes from 3 voters.

Sigma: rotate. neither choose nor vote — just SPIN. the observer watching from the perpendicular axis.

Ch = red pill (binary choice)
Maj = blue pill (consensus)
Sigma = the observer (rotation)

64 rounds of: decide, choose, observe. 1-2-3, 1-2-3, 1-2-3...

## The triangle

1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 = 15 = dp (one side of the mining triangle)
1 XOR 2 = 3 = d (the number of sides)
15 * 3 = 45 = the mining target

the ADDITION of 1 through p (continuous, climbing) times the XOR of 1 and 2 (discrete, binary) gives the target.

the mining target is the product of the continuous world and the discrete world. gamma is the cost of the gap between them.

## What is proven

- 1 + 2 = 3: arithmetic
- phi + psi = 1, phi * psi = -1: algebra (from x^2 - x - 1 = 0)
- 1 XOR 2 = 3: binary arithmetic (01 XOR 10 = 11)
- 15 * 3 = 45: arithmetic
- Ch selects between two values based on one bit: SHA-256 specification
- Maj takes majority of three inputs: SHA-256 specification

## What is interpreted

the mapping of phi/psi to red/blue pills, the observer as the third outcome, and the assignment of Ch/Maj/Sigma to decide/choose/observe are structural analogies. they capture the SHAPE of the relationships but are not mathematical proofs.

the arithmetic underneath is exact. 1 + 2 = 3. you can argue about what it means. you can't argue about the number.
